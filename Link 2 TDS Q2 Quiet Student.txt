Q2. Quiet student

Write an SQL query to report the students (student_id, student_name) being 
“quiet” in ALL exams. A “quite” student is the one who took at least one exam and didn’t score neither 
the high score nor the low score.				      

Method 1:
select student_id, student_name from
(
select student_id, student_name, score, max(score) over(partition by exam_id) as max_score, min(score) over(partition by exam_id) as min_score
from student s join exam e
on s.student_id= exam.student_id
) t1
where (score!=max_score or score!=min_score)

Method 2: 
Select student_id, name 

(Select student.student_id, name, exam_id, score from student join
Exam on student.student_id=exam.student_id) t1
Join

(Select exam_id, max(Score) as max_Score, min(score) as min_score
From exam) t2
On t1. exam_id= t2.exam_id
And (t1.score!= max_score or t1.score!=min_score)


Python:

df= student.merge(exam, how=‘inner’, on=’student_id)
exam_t= exam.groupby([‘exam_id’], as_index=‘False).agg(min_score=(‘score’,’min’), max_score=(‘score’, ‘max’))
df_merged=df.merge(exam_t, how=‘inner’, on=‘exam_id’)
df_merged.loc[df[‘score’]!=df[‘max_score’] | df[‘score’]!=df[‘min_score’], [’student_id’,’student_name’]]
